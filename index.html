Tense Conjugation Rules

All conjugations here use the standard polite informal style (아요/어요), perfect for everyday communication.

1. Present Tense (-아요/어요)

• If verb stem ends in ㅏ or ㅗ → add -아요

• For other vowels → add -어요

• 하다 verbs change to 해요

2. Past Tense (-았어요/었어요)

• Same vowel rules as present tense, but insert the double 'ㅆ' bottom consonant.

• e.g., 알다 → 알아 + 았어요 = 알았어요

3. Future Tense (-(으)ㄹ 거예요)

• Stem ends in vowel → add -ㄹ 거예요

• Stem ends in consonant → add -을 거예요

4. Present Progressive (-고 있어요)

• Simply drop 다 and attach -고 있어요 to the stem.

• Means "am/is/are doing".

5. Past Progressive (-고 있었어요)

• Drop 다 and attach -고 있었어요 to the stem.

• Means "was/were doing".

6. Future Progressive (-고 있을 거예요)

• Drop 다 and attach -고 있을 거예요 to the stem.

• Means "will be doing".
